Fishing Planet
Technologies Unity, C#
Fishing Planet is a realistic first-person fishing simulator, it is available on PC (Steam), Playstation 4 and XBox One. My areas of responsibility on this project are client core-gameplay mechanics and physics. I've coded fishing FSM, physical behavior of rod, line and tackle, procedural animation of fish, movement of kayaks and motorboats. I also was experimenting with SteamVR to run the game in VR.

Videos below show examples of procedural fish animation: the left one was used in trailer for PS4 release, the right one is physically simulated fish behavior used in gameplay.
Due to specifics of fishing mechanics, Unity's built-in physics engine turned out to be unsuitable for this task. In particular, it couldn't handle large difference in mass of connected objects properly. In the case of "fish-line-rod" system it can reach several orders of magnitude. Therefore, it was decided to do a custom solution which runs in separate thread and only relies on Unity collision detection. It was an additional challenge to optimize this system for the consoles hardware.

Boat simulation shown in videos below uses realistic calculation of hydrostatic force as well as simplified vessel hydrodynamics. Force exerted by a paddle during rowing in kayak is calculated based on character's hands movement and then applied to the vessel.
I had been working on VR version for a few months as an experiment just to see what our game would look like through a HMD. I managed to attach the fishing rod physics to the controller and implement throwing (or casting), but the most difficult part was to move the UI to world-space and make it work with "laser pointer" instead of mouse. Here is a video from one of our beta-testers showing fishing in VR. Keep in mind that it wasn't production-ready version and we had abandoned VR eventually (at least for now).

Procedural GPGPU generation of planets and asteroids
Technologies Unity, C#, DirectCompute, HLSL
This tool was developed as a part of my space simulator pet project. It is capable of generating models of astronomical objects in real-time using only user's resources. The process is basically a pipeline of parallel CPU threads with DirectCompute GPGPU computations.
The body surface is a geosphere with altered vertices heights, derived from an icosahedron by multiple tesselation passes. Surface noise can be calculated by several Perlin FBM (Fractal Brownian Motion) based algorithms. Each pair of triangle sides of the icosahedron is forming a pseudo-rectangular region, for each of these regions a color texture and a normal map are generated with the same algorithm.
The atmosphere is rendered as inside-out geosphere with the shader which interpolates two colors depending on length of view ray intersection with the atmosphere.
Tesselation, mesh building and textures colorizing are calculated on parallel CPU threads; surface noise, color textures and normal maps are generated on GPU. The whole process for body of 700K triangles, ten 1024x1024 textures and normals maps takes about 15 seconds on modern desktop PC.
